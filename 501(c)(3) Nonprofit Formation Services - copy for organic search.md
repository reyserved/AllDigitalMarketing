## **What is a 501(c)(3) organization?**

A 501(c)(3) is a nonprofit organization that qualifies for tax-exempt status under **Section 501(c)(3)** of the Internal Revenue Code. This status provides numerous benefits, including:

* [**Tax exemptions**](https://chisholmfirm.com/nonprofit-formation-services/state-tax-exemption/) at federal and state levels  
* **Eligibility for grants** from foundations and government agencies  
* **Donor trust** through official nonprofit recognition, enabling tax-deductible donations

At Chisholm Law Firm, we help charities, schools, churches, and other organizations achieve 501(c)(3) status. Proper [nonprofit planning](https://chisholmfirm.com/nonprofit-formation-services/nonprofit-planning/) ensures your organization is structured to achieve long-term goals and mission success. Our attorneys assist clients across a wide range of sectors, including:

## **Understanding the Key Differences Between Nonprofit Types**

Additional nonprofit organization types to consider:

| Nonprofit Type | Purpose | Tax-Deductible Donations | Political Activity | Common Examples |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 501(c)(3) | Charitable, educational, religious, etc. | Yes | No | Charities, Churches, Schools |
| 501(c)(4) | Social welfare, advocacy, and lobbying | No | Yes | Advocacy Groups, Lobbying Organizations |
| 501(c)(6) | Business leagues, chambers of commerce | No | Limited | Trade Associations, Professional Societies |
| 501(c)(7) | Social clubs (recreational activities) | No | Limited | Country Clubs, Hobby Clubs |
