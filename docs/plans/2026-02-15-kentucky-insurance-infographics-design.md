## Infographic 1: Kentucky Auto Insurance Minimums Explained

### Purpose
Replace the basic 25/50/25 text display with a comprehensive reference guide explaining Kentucky's mandatory auto insurance requirements in a scannable, authoritative format.

### Sections

#### Section 1: Hero Header Panel
- **Layout:** Full-width top banner
- **Background:** Brand dark (#1D252D) with subtle gradient
- **Content:**
  - Headline: "Kentucky Auto Insurance Requirements"
  - Subhead: "Choice No-Fault State | Mandatory Minimum Coverage"
  - Source badge: "Source: Kentucky Transportation Cabinet"
- **Visuals:** Kentucky state outline icon, clean sans-serif typography

#### Section 2: The 25/50/25 Hero Display
- **Layout:** Central dominant panel
- **Background:** White with brand red (#C92A39) border
- **Content:**
  - Large display: "25 / 50 / 25"
  - Label: "State Minimum Liability Limits"
  - Three-column breakdown below: "$25K Per Person | $50K Per Accident | $25K Property Damage"
- **Visuals:** Bold numbers (4rem), brand red accent color

#### Section 3: Three-Column Coverage Breakdown
- **Layout:** Three equal columns
- **Column 1 - Bodily Injury Per Person:**
  - Icon: Person/user icon
  - Amount: $25,000
  - Description: Maximum coverage for each individual injured in an accident you cause
  - Example: Covers medical expenses, rehabilitation, lost wages
- **Column 2 - Bodily Injury Per Accident:**
  - Icon: Group/people icon
  - Amount: $50,000
  - Description: Total coverage for all injuries in a single accident
  - Example: If 3 people are injured, $50,000 splits among all claims
- **Column 3 - Property Damage:**
  - Icon: Car/home icon
  - Amount: $25,000
  - Description: Damage to another person's vehicle or property
  - Example: Repairs to another driver's car, fence, mailbox, building
- **Visuals:** Consistent card styling, icons in brand red, bold amounts

#### Section 4: PIP Requirement Panel
- **Layout:** Full-width panel below columns
- **Background:** Light accent (#f8f9fa)
- **Content:**
  - Header: "Personal Injury Protection (PIP)"
  - Minimum Required: $10,000
  - Bullets: Covers medical expenses, lost wages regardless of fault
  - Note: Required in Kentucky's no-fault system
- **Visuals:** Shield icon, highlighted "$10,000" amount

#### Section 5: Real-World Coverage Scenario
- **Layout:** Bottom panel with scenario example
- **Content:**
  - Scenario: You cause an accident injuring two people and damaging a parked car
  - Breakdown: Person 1 ($18K) + Person 2 ($22K) + Property ($8K) = $48K
  - Result: Your 25/50/25 coverage pays ✓ $48,000 (You pay $0)
- **Visuals:** Checkmark in green, numerical breakdown

### Authoritative Data Sources
- Kentucky Transportation Cabinet (kytc.ky.gov)
- Kentucky Department of Insurance

## Infographic 2: Kentucky Home Insurance Risk Guide

### Purpose
Fill the empty placeholder with a comprehensive guide to Kentucky-specific weather risks and appropriate insurance coverage, helping homeowners understand what protection they need.

### Sections

#### Section 1: Hero Header Panel
- **Layout:** Full-width top banner
- **Background:** Brand dark (#1D252D) with subtle gradient
- **Content:**
  - Headline: "Common Kentucky Home Insurance Risks"
  - Subhead: "Protect Your Home From Weather-Related Damage"
  - Source badge: "Source: National Weather Service | FEMA"
- **Visuals:** Home/shield icon, clean typography matching Infographic 1

#### Section 2: Kentucky Map Context Panel
- **Layout:** Central visual panel with stylized map
- **Background:** White with subtle gray border
- **Content:**
  - Simplified Kentucky state outline with risk zone shading
  - Legend: Yellow (Moderate), Orange (High Flood Areas), Red (Tornado Regions)
  - Label: "Know Your Local Risk Factors"
- **Visuals:** Stylized map (not detailed flood zone map), warm tone color coding

#### Section 3: Three Risk Cards (Grid Layout)

**Risk Card 1: Flooding**
- **Icon:** Water wave icon in brand red
- **Risk Level:** HIGH (red badge)
- **Content:**
  - Types: Flash flooding, River flooding, Urban drainage
  - Kentucky Fact: "Eastern Kentucky experienced historic flooding in July 2022 causing $1B+ damage"
  - Protection Needed: Separate flood insurance policy, NFIP coverage, Sump pump backup
- **Source:** FEMA, NWS

**Risk Card 2: Tornadoes**
- **Icon:** Tornado/spiral icon in brand red
- **Risk Level:** MODERATE (orange badge)
- **Content:**
  - Context: Kentucky is part of "Hoosier Alley" - active tornado region
  - Kentucky Fact: "Average of 21 tornadoes per year statewide"
  - Protection Needed: Wind & hail coverage, Roof reinforcement, Safe room/shelter, Impact-resistant windows
- **Source:** National Weather Service

**Risk Card 3: Winter Storms**
- **Icon:** Snowflake/ice crystal icon in brand red
- **Risk Level:** MODERATE (orange badge)
- **Content:**
  - Types: Ice storms, Heavy snow, Freezing rain
  - Kentucky Fact: "Ice storms cause extended power outages and roof collapse from weight"
  - Protection Needed: Generator coverage, Food spoilage protection, Alternative heat source coverage
- **Source:** National Weather Service, Kentucky Climate Center

#### Section 4: Action Prompt Panel
- **Layout:** Full-width panel at bottom
- **Background:** Light accent (#f8f9fa) with brand red left border
- **Content:**
  - Alert: "Standard homeowners policies EXCLUDE flood damage"
  - Statistic: "1 in 4 Kentucky flood claims come from outside mapped high-risk zones"
  - Action Items:
    - Consider adding separate flood insurance (NFIP)
    - Review wind/hail coverage limits
    - Document home contents for faster claims
- **Visuals:** Alert icon, arrow icons for action items

#### Section 5: Footer Source Panel
- **Layout:** Narrow strip at bottom
- **Content:** "Data sources: National Weather Service (weather.gov) | FEMA (fema.gov) | Kentucky Climate Center"
- **Visuals:** Divider line, grayscale text

### Authoritative Data Sources
- National Weather Service (weather.gov)
- Federal Emergency Management Agency (fema.gov)
- Kentucky Climate Center (kyclimate.org)
