A Framework for Bear Market Readiness

A practical four-step framework designed to support decision quality during periods of market stress. Each step focuses on aligning liquidity, risk exposure, taxes, and behavior with your long-term objectives.

1

Protect Liquidity

Build a 12–24 month liquidity strategy so near-term obligations can be met without unnecessary disruption to long-term investment allocations.

2

Reconfirm Risk

Reassess portfolio concentration, key drivers of return, and time horizon alignment to confirm your risk exposure reflects both your goals and capacity.

3

Coordinate Taxes and Cash Flow

Review realized gains, losses, and expected cash needs with your advisor and CPA before implementing strategy adjustments.

4

Install Behavioral Guardrails

Establish written decision rules and review protocols so short-term stress does not lead to unplanned strategy changes.

Before, During, and After Volatility

Use this structured checklist approach to support disciplined decision-making during evolving market conditions.

Before Volatility Checklist

Prepare in advance so decisions are guided by a documented plan rather than short-term headlines.

  • Confirm a 12 to 24 month liquidity plan for known obligations.
  • Review concentration and diversification exposures across accounts.
  • Document rebalancing bands, cadence, and implementation rules.
  • Complete no-regret planning tasks and update key records.
  • Establish review cadence and communication protocols.

Phase Preview

During volatility, revisit liquidity first and document the rationale for any material strategy adjustments.

After volatility, evaluate allocation drift, reassess liquidity reserves, and update your written plan as appropriate.

Decision discipline means strategy changes are tied to goals, time horizon, and cash needs — not short-term market movements.

Decision Filter and One-Page Action Plan

Apply a consistent decision framework before making material strategy adjustments. Durable changes are grounded in your personal circumstances, not short-term market sentiment.

Decision Filter

Use this sequence before making any major portfolio change during a bear market period.

  1. Did your life circumstances change in a material way?
  2. Did your goals or time horizon change?
  3. Are you responding primarily to fear, headlines, or short-term forecasts?
  4. Would this strategy still align with your objectives if markets declined further?

Prepare for Advisor and CPA Conversations

Enter each meeting with focused questions that connect investment strategy, tax considerations, and near-term cash-flow planning.

Questions for Your Advisor

  • How resilient is my liquidity plan under multiple stress scenarios?
  • Where is concentration risk most significant today, and how should it be addressed over time?
  • What rebalancing policy will be followed if volatility increases?
  • Which portfolio changes should be deferred until risk and tax impact are fully reviewed?
  • Which behavioral guardrails should be formalized now?

Questions for Your CPA

  • What is my year-to-date realized gain and loss profile?
  • Would tax-loss harvesting be appropriate given my specific tax profile and circumstances?
  • How should charitable plans be coordinated for current-year tax impact?
  • Do upcoming tax payments change near-term liquidity requirements?
  • Which timing decisions this year carry the largest tax sensitivity?
