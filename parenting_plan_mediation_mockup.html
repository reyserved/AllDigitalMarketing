How Arizona Law Supports Parenting Plan Mediation

Arizona law encourages families to resolve parenting matters peacefully. Under Arizona Revised Statutes § 25-403, the state prioritizes the "best interests of the child," recognizing that collaborative agreements often serve children better than court-imposed orders. Mediation allows parents to reach their own settlement outside the courtroom.

Mediation agreements that meet Arizona’s requirements can be submitted to the court and entered as legally binding orders. Communications during mediation are protected under Rule 67.3 of the Arizona Rules of Family Law Procedure, meaning your discussions remain confidential.

Why Arizona Courts Encourage Mediation

Arizona judges often recommend or order mediation before a custody trial under Rule 68 of the Arizona Rules of Family Law Procedure. State policy promotes mediation to:

Reduce Congestion

Reduces court congestion and lowers costs to taxpayers by keeping cases out of the system.

Support Cooperation

Supports cooperation between parents specifically for the benefit and well-being of children.

Honor Agreements

Fosters agreements that families are more likely to honor because they created them together.

Decrease Conflict

Decreases post-divorce conflict and minimizes the need for repeated court motions.

Developing a Comprehensive Parenting Plan

Our experienced mediators guide you through every detail to ensure your plan is robust and practical. We assist you in addressing:

1. The Regular Parenting Schedule

We help you design a routine that provides stability for your children and fits your work and lifestyle. Whether it's a 5-2-2-5, week-on/week-off, or a custom schedule, we find what works best for you.

2. Holidays and Vacations

Holiday schedules often supersede the regular routine. We help you proactively plan for major holidays (Thanksgiving, Christmas/Hanukkah, etc.), school breaks, and family vacations to avoid last-minute stress.

3. Decision-Making Authority

We facilitate agreements on how you will make significant decisions. Most clients in mediation choose Joint Legal Decision-Making, where parents consult and agree on major issues, but we can structure this to fit your specific circumstances.

4. Transition & Logistics

The small details matter. We help you define how exchanges will occur, who provides transportation, and how to handle children's belongings, ensuring smooth transitions between homes.
